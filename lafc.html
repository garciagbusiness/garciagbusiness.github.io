The Assignment

The Brief

LAFC asked candidates to complete a take-home project mirroring the actual day-to-day work of the role: analyze a proprietary post-event fan survey dataset from BMO Stadium, identify key patterns and opportunities, and deliver a comprehensive 5-year business plan (2026–2030) to be presented directly to the Club President.

The deliverables included a full analytics dashboard, a business plan presentation, and a 10-minute video walkthrough defending every recommendation with specific data points. No restrictions on tools — candidates were expected to use whatever they'd use on the job.

My guiding principle throughout: every recommendation must be anchored to a data point. Nothing based on gut feeling.

Analysis

Key Findings

Four data-driven insights that shaped the entire business strategy.

01

The Quality-Over-Quantity Dynamic

Average fan spend grew from $206 in 2021 to $441 in 2025 — a 114% increase — even as survey volume dropped after the 2022 championship peak. The fans who stayed were more engaged and higher-spending. This isn't a problem; it's a foundation to build on.

$206 → $441 Avg. spend per fan · 2021–2025
Strategy must capture more value from existing high-quality fans while rebuilding volume.
02

The Premium Revenue Gap

VIP and Floor/Pit fans make up just 21.8% of attendees but generate 43% of all captured revenue. A VIP fan spends $573 per visit vs. $240 for General Admission — a 2.4x multiplier per seat assignment.

$573 vs $240 VIP vs General Admission avg. spend
Converting just 5% of GA fans to Premium adds ~$110K in revenue without adding a single new ticket.
03

Fan Friction = Lost Revenue

5,410 fans left open-ended feedback. 72% described a problem, not a compliment. The top friction points — F&B wait times (871 mentions), parking (749 mentions), and entry lines (1,214 combined mentions) — are not soft complaints. They are direct revenue leaks.

72% of fan feedback described a problem
A fan who skips the food line spends $60–$80 less per visit. These friction points have a clear dollar value.
04

The Membership Paradox

The most surprising finding: non-members outspend LAFC members by 92% ($535 vs $279 per visit). Members are not being incentivized to spend more on match day — no rewards, no spend thresholds, no upgrade programs.

$535 vs $279 Non-member vs member avg. spend
Members are LAFC's most loyal fans but behave like budget fans on match day. Membership redesign is overdue.
Business Plan

The 3-Pillar Strategy

A sequenced framework — you cannot build premium programs on top of a broken baseline experience.

01

Fix the Experience

2026 — Foundation

  • Mobile ordering via LAFC app — eliminate the F&B line barrier
  • Combo bundle pricing ($15–20) to reduce sticker shock
  • Pre-paid parking bundles in season ticket packages
  • Metro partnership for staggered transit exit credits
  • Member-only fast-track entry lanes
KPI Targets: F&B complaints <8% · Entry wait <8 min · Parking complaints <7%
02

Grow Premium Revenue

2026–2029 — Multi-Year Build

  • 2026: Premium Day Pass — upgrade any ticket to Floor/Pit for select matches
  • 2027: In-seat F&B service for Floor/Pit tiers (+$75/fan target)
  • 2028: Restructure inventory based on 2 years of demand data
  • 2029: Platinum Membership tier ($5K–$10K/year) with curated events
KPI Targets: 5% GA-to-Premium conversion · $75 incremental spend/premium fan
03

Reactivate & Retain

2026–2027 — CRM Infrastructure

  • Win-back campaign targeting ~2,300 dormant 2022 fans — goal: 600 re-activations
  • Membership redesign: Silver / Gold / Platinum tiers — reward spend, not just attendance
  • Points on F&B, priority upgrade windows, exclusive events for top members
  • CRM integration: Ticketmaster + POS + Salesforce — 60% of fans with linked profiles by 2027
KPI Targets: 600 re-activations in 2026 · 30% of members upgrade to Gold/Platinum by 2027
